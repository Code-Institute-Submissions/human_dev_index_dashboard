Introduction

The HDI was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, not economic growth alone. The HDI study measures achievment within three primary dimensions and their correlating indicators; Long and Happy Life (Life Expectancy), knowledge (education) and standard of living (Gross National Income). The HDI simplifies and captures only part of what human development entails. This study does not reflect on inequalities or poverty.
