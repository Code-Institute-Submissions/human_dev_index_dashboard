Introduction

The HDI was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, not economic growth alone. The HDI study measures achievment within three primary dimensions and their correlating indicators; Long and Happy Life (Life Expectancy), knowledge (education) and standard of living (Gross National Income). The HDI simplifies and captures only part of what human development entails. This study does not reflect on inequalities or poverty.

Findings



This chart indicates that the highest levels of education correlate to higher HDI scores. There is also a correlation between a low number of education years and low GNI, which together result in a lower than average HDI score.



This chart indicates that GNI levels falling into the median range correlate to the highest HDI scores, while the lowest GNI levels correlate to the lowest HDI scores.



This chart indicates that a higher life expectancy at birth correlates to a higher HDI score.
